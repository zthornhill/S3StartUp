Calorie Tracker Formula & Arithmetic

The Calorie Tracker Calculator takes multiple factors into account when determining results. By utilizing Mifflin-St Jeor and Harris-Benedict formulas, the Calorie Tracker Calculator can accurately obtain results for body mass index and metalbolic rate(s) - amount of energy expended.

Calorie Tracker For Men

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation : 10W + 6.25H - 5A + 5
Harris-Benedict Equation : 13.397W + 4.799H - 5.677A + 88.362

Calorie Tracker For Women

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation : 10W + 6.25H - 5A - 161
Harris-Benedict Equation : 9.247W + 3.098H - 4.330A + 447.593 ** NOTE ** :
W = Weight in kilograms (kg)
H = Height in centimeters (cm)
A = Age

Why these formulas?

The value obtained from these formulas and equations is the estimated number of calories a person can or should consume in a day to maintain a healthy and nutritious body-weight, diet, and more. The value is obtained by multiplying it by an activity, which depends on a individual's amount of exercise, in order to find an optimal and realistic value for maintaining a healthy body-weight, diet, etc.

Remember that eating properly and exercise are key and widely accepted as the best method to lose weight. Calorie Tracker will not recommend or advise anyone lower their calorie intake by 1,000 calories or more a day. Doing so would result in losing more than 2 pounds per week and that can be unhealthy and give the opposite effect of reducing metabolism. Losing more than 2 lbs per week results in muscle loss, lower basal metabolic rate, dehydration, and more. Maintaining a balance and healthy diet is crucial, the body needs to be able to support the metabolic process(es) and replenish itself. Without the proper nutrients, the body can have negative long term effects. Not only is monitoring calorie intake necessary, but its also important to maintain the proper levels of fiber and other nutrition's to balance the bodies needs.
